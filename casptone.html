Capstone Project

Urban Lawn Area Quantification in the City of North Vancouver

This capstone project analyzes the spatial distribution and total coverage of urban lawns within the City of North Vancouver. Lawns represent a major component of urban green infrastructure and can influence biodiversity, water use, and ecological processes in rapidly developing cities.

Objectives

  • Quantify total lawn area in the City of North Vancouver
  • Analyze lawn distribution across different land-use zones
  • Assess the role of lawns as urban green infrastructure

Data

  • High-resolution aerial imagery
  • Municipal land-use zoning data
  • Validation points for classification accuracy

Methods

Remote sensing techniques were used to classify lawn areas from aerial imagery. A Random Forest classification model was applied within ArcGIS Pro to distinguish lawn and non-lawn land cover types. Vegetation indices derived from RGB imagery helped enhance the detection of turfgrass areas. Zonal statistics were then used to calculate lawn coverage within each land-use zone of the municipality.

Results

The analysis produced a detailed spatial map of lawn distribution and quantified the total lawn area within the municipality. Results show that lawns occupy a substantial proportion of residential and park land-use zones, highlighting their importance in urban green infrastructure and ecological planning.

Conclusion
